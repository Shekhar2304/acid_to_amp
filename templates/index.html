The Science of Bioelectric Remediation

Exoelectrogenic Mechanism

Shewanella oneidensis MR-1 and Geobacter sulfurreducens form electroactive biofilms on anode surfaces, transferring electrons via:

Direct electron transfer (c-type cytochromes)
Mediated transfer (endogenous flavins)
Nanowire conductive pili
Electron transfer rate: 3.2 × 10⁶ e⁻/cell/s

Acid Mine Drainage Chemistry

Pyrite oxidation in Jharkhand coal seams:

2FeS₂ + 7O₂ + 2H₂O → 2Fe²⁺ + 4SO₄²⁻ + 4H⁺
4Fe²⁺ + O₂ + 4H⁺ → 4Fe³⁺ + 2H₂O (microbial catalysis)
